# Initial Exploratory Data Analysis Findings

## Overview

Initial exploratory data analysis was performed on the M5 Forecasting dataset to understand the product hierarchy, store coverage, and product pricing distribution.

## Product Distribution by Category

The FOODS category contains the highest number of unique products, followed by HOUSEHOLD and HOBBIES.

This indicates that the dataset's product portfolio is not evenly distributed across categories. Food products represent the largest product group.

## Product Distribution by Department

The FOODS_3 department contains the highest number of unique products.

The number of products varies significantly across departments, indicating differences in product diversity within the retail hierarchy.

## Product Records by Store

The dataset contains approximately the same number of product records for each of the ten stores.

This indicates consistent product-level dataset coverage across stores.

The current visualization represents product records and does not measure total sales performance. Store-level sales performance will be analyzed separately.

## Sell Price Distribution

The sell price distribution is strongly right-skewed.

Most price observations are concentrated in the lower price range, while a small number of products have significantly higher prices.

The initial dataset summary showed an average sell price of approximately $4.41 and a maximum observed sell price of $107.32.

## Business Insights

- Food products represent the largest portion of the product portfolio.
- Product diversity varies across departments.
- Product-level dataset coverage is consistent across stores.
- Most retail price observations are concentrated in lower price ranges.
- High-priced observations may require further investigation during price analysis.
